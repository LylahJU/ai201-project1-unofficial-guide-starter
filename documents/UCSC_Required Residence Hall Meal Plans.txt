Required Residence Hall Meal Plans


Flexible plans and delicious food
Our meal plans are designed to work with your lifestyle. Whether you’re meeting friends at a cafe, enjoying a cozy meal in a dining hall, or picking up snacks at a market with roommates, so much of campus life happens around the table.


If you live in a residence hall, a meal plan is part of your housing contract – you’ll need to select one when you apply for housing. This page includes detailed meal plan information, plus a comparison chart to help you choose a plan that fits your dining preferences and schedule. Slug appétit!




Not living in a residence hall?
If you live in a campus apartment or off campus, you don’t have a required meal plan. You can choose to purchase Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars for convenience and discounts at UCSC Dining locations. Learn more on our Voluntary Meal Plans page.


Unlimited Meal Plans
NEW for the 2025-26 academic year
Unlimited Meal Plans offer unlimited entry for dine-in service at our all-you-care-to-enjoy dining halls – so you can visit a dining hall for a snack or a cup of coffee (and skip the line at the cafe) without worrying about using up a meal swipe. With no need to track points or budget meals, you can focus on fueling your day without added stress. Unlimited Plans also come with 75 Slug Points per quarter, which can be used to make purchases at campus cafes, markets, and coffee bars, to pay for Eco-Box meals to-go, or to pay for a guest at a dining hall. Slug Points reset at the beginning of each quarter.

How to use your meal plan
No matter which plan you choose, meal plans are easy to use:


Swipe your student ID or use the GET mobile app to enter a dining hall or make a purchase in a cafe, market, or coffee bar. 
Get a 10% discount on orders from cafes, markets, and coffee bars when you pay with Slug Points, Banana Bucks, or Flexi Dollars.
Meal plans reset at the beginning of each quarter. If your Slug Points run low, you can add optional Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars anytime through the GET app.


Filing periods for meal plan changes 2025-26
Submit your meal plan change request in the Student Housing Portal during the filing periods listed below. If you’re satisfied with your current plan, no action is required.


May 1 through September 15, 2025        Filing period for meal plan changes effective fall quarter.
November 1-15, 2025        Filing period for meal plan changes effective winter quarter.
February 1-15, 2026        Filing period for meal plan changes effective spring quarter. 
Mid-quarter changes
Unlimited plans: You can request an upgrade (for example, from 5-Day to 7-Day Unlimited) by emailing meals@ucsc.edu. Downgrades are not allowed mid-quarter.
Slug Points plans: Changes are not permitted mid-quarter. If your balance is running low, you can add Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars anytime using the GET app or website.


Frequently Asked Questions
Meal plan basics
Am I required to get a meal plan?
A meal plan is required for students living in the residence halls and is part of the housing contract. A meal plan will only be applied to your bill in my.ucsc.edu if you are assigned to housing in a residence hall.


How do I sign up for a meal plan?
You will choose a meal plan in the Housing Portal when you apply for university housing.


Which meal plan should I choose?
Consider how often you plan to eat on campus:


Will you be eating three meals a day, seven days a week at UCSC Dining locations?
Do you usually eat breakfast?
Do you like to have snacks during the day?
There is no right or wrong meal plan; choose the plan that best fits your lifestyle and budget. Take a quick quiz to see which meal plan best fits your lifestyle!


Can I purchase a meal plan even if I’m not living in a residence hall?
Students living in campus apartments, ARCH communities, and off-campus can purchase Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars using the GET app or website. Both Banana Bucks and Flexi Dollars provide discounts at all UCSC Dining-operated locations on campus. Flexi$ can also be used at select off-campus vendors, and for delivery from local restaurants via Grubhub.


Using your meal plan
How many meals do I get per week?
Unlimited Meal Plans offer unlimited entry for dine-in service at our all-you-care-to-enjoy dining halls. Choose from weekday-only or 7-day entry options. Unlimited Plans also come with $75 Slug Points per quarter. 


Slug Points Meal Plans offer flexible spending at any UCSC Dining location—from dining halls to cafes, coffee bars, and markets. With a Slug Point plan, each dining hall meal is one simple price—for all-you-care-to-enjoy—which works out to about 10 meals/week on the Blue plan, about 14 meals/week on the Gold plan, and about 19 meals/week on the Banana Slug plan. You can also use any Slug Points Meal Plan to purchase items a-la-carte (prices vary) from cafes, markets, and coffee bars all across campus.


Where can I use my meal plan?
Unlimited Meal Plans can be used at any Dining Hall. Slug Points Meal Plans can be used at all UCSC Dining locations, including Dining Halls, cafes, coffee bars, and markets all across campus. Enjoy a 10% discount when you pay with Slug Points at campus cafes, markets, and coffee bars. Unlimited plans come with 75 Slug Points per quarter that can be used for occasional purchases at UCSC Dining cafes and markets.


Can I use my meal plan off-campus?
You cannot use your Unlimited Meal Plan or Slug Points Meal Plan off-campus. However, you can purchase Flexi Dollars (i.e., Flexi$) anytime by using the GET app or website. Flexi$ can be used with your student ID card at our off-campus partner businesses, or in the GrubHub app for delivery and pickup from many local restaurants. Flexi$ can also be spent at all campus dining locations. Family members can also add funds for their student through the GET website.


How can I check my meal plan balance?
If you have a Slug Points Meal Plan, you can check your balance and transactions anytime with the GET app or website. If you have an Unlimited plan, the GET app will show “Meals: 0” because there’s an unlimited number of dining hall entries on your account—not a set number; however, you can track the balance for the Slug Points portion of your plan (Unlimited plans come with $75 Slug Points per quarter).


If you add optional Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars to your account, you can also check your balance for those through the GET app or website.


What’s the difference between Slug Points and Banana Bucks?
Both Slug Points and Banana Bucks are accepted at all locations operated by UCSC Dining. Residence hall meal plans come with Slug Points. Slug Points expire at the end of each quarter. Banana Bucks are points purchased through the GET mobile app or website to add to your account when your Slug Points are running low. Banana Bucks carry over quarter to quarter and expire at the end of the academic year in June (June 15, 2026). Banana Bucks cannot be used until you’ve used up all of the Slug Points in your Residence Hall meal plan for the quarter.


Do unused Slug Points carry over to the next quarter?
Meal plans reset at the beginning of each quarter and unused Slug Points do not carry over into the following quarter nor are they reimbursed. Students are encouraged to make full use of their meal plan each quarter, and to change their plan if necessary during the published change periods to ensure they are on a plan that best meets their needs.


Managing your plan
Can I add more points to my meal plan if I run out?
Yes! If your Slug Points balance begins to run low, you can add Banana Bucks at any time during the quarter using the GET app or website. Families can also add funds for their student through the GET website (get.cbord.com/ucsc). Banana Bucks can be purchased in increments of 50, 100, and 500*. For additional flexibility, Flexi Dollars can also be added through GET. Flexi Dollars can be used at all campus dining locations, as well as the Bay Tree Campus Store, select off-campus vendors, and for food delivery from local restaurants through Grubhub. Banana Bucks and Flexi Dollars added through GET carry over from quarter to quarter and expire at the end of the academic year in June (June 15, 2026)


Unlimited Meal Plans come with 75 Slug Points per quarter, and you can add Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars the GET app. 


*Note: The 500 Banana Bucks option is not available to add after the end of April. The 100 Banana Bucks option is not available to add after mid-May. The 50 Banana Bucks option is not available to add after early June.


I picked a plan that is too large/too small – can I change it?
During the summer, and during published change periods in fall and winter quarters, you can change your meal plan for the following quarter. You can switch between plan tiers or move from a Slug Points meal plan to an Unlimited meal plan and vice versa, depending on your needs.


Can I upgrade my meal plan in the middle of the quarter?
Mid-quarter meal plan changes are limited. If you are on a Slug Points meal plan and your balance begins to run low, you can add Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars using the GET app or website. To request a mid-quarter upgrade from your Unlimited 5-Day Meal Plan to an Unlimited 7-Day Meal Plan, you can email meals@ucsc.edu.
Please note: Students cannot downgrade their meal plan mid-quarter. Instead, you can change your meal plan during the next published change period.


Family and account questions
Can my parents/family members add more points to my meal plan?
Yes! Parents, guardians, and other relatives can add Banana Bucks or Flexi Dollars to a student account on the GET website (get.cbord.com/ucsc). No login is required; however, your family member will need to know your UCSC student ID number in order to add funds to your account.


Can my parents/family see how many Slug Points I have left?
Your parents/family members can add funds to your account using the GET website, but they cannot view your Slug Points balance. State and federal laws prevent the disclosure of your financial information to any third party (even your parent or guardian) without your consent. If you would like to let someone know how many Slug Points you have left, you can check your balance in the GET app and let them know.


Additional questions
Can I share my Unlimited Plan, Slug Points or Banana Bucks with another student?
Unlimited Plans, Slug Points, Banana Bucks, and Flexi Dollars are non-transferable and non-refundable. But you can treat a friend to a meal using your Slug Points, Banana Bucks, or Flexi Dollars at any UCSC Dining location!


Can I get my money back if I don’t use my meal plan?
No, meal plans are non-transferable and non-refundable. Slug Points expire at the end of each quarter. Students are encouraged to make full use of their meal plan each quarter, and to change their plan if necessary during the published change periods to ensure they are on a plan that best meets their needs. If you would like to change to a different meal plan level, you can do that during the summer and during specified filing periods in fall and winter quarters for the following quarter.


Banana Bucks and Flexi Dollars are non-transferable and non-refundable. They carry over from quarter to quarter and expire at the end of the academic year in June. 


I have dietary restrictions. Am I able to have the requirement for a meal plan waived?
No; students living in the residence halls are required to have a meal plan as part of their housing contract. While the meal plan cannot be waived, we understand that every student’s needs are unique. If your dietary needs aren’t being met through regular dining options, we encourage you to connect with Dining’s Food Safety and Nutrition Specialist. They’re here to listen, understand your concerns, and work with you to find solutions that support your well-being.
